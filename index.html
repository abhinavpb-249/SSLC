How is it Calculated?

Each subject is assigned a grade point like this:
Grade Grade point
A+ 9
A 8
B+ 7
B 6
C+ 5
C 4
D+ 3
D 2
E 1


Then the Total Grade Point (TGP) is calculated by adding the grade point of all 10 subjects.
For example, if a student got 10 A+, their grade point is $10 \times 9 = 90 $.
This should be equivalent to 100%.

The relation can be expressed as $$ 100 = 90 \times \frac{100}{90} $$ Generally, $$ Percentage = TGP \times \frac{100}{90} $$ This site calculates TGP as:

TGP = (9 x No. of A+) + (8 x No. of A)+... + (1 x No. of E)



However remember that given result is only an approximation and the value may be different by maximum 10 percentage.
To get accurate result, apply for marklist

if you have your marks,you can get the accurate percentage as: $$\textnormal{Percentage=}\frac{\textnormal{marks obtained}}{\textnormal{Total marks}} \times 100$$
But your marks are not usually given with your SSLC result. So, to get them you have to "Demand Draft" for it from pareekshabhavan
